Animal Rights in India

What do you say when you are in pain?
Ouch perhaps?
but have you ever thought about what would these voiceless animals say when they are in pain?

But hopefully, the Indian government has established certain laws and acts that prevent animal cruelty.

Everyone holds
a duty of being
compassionate and
empathise with all the
living creatures.



To kill any animal
including stray
animals
is a punishable
offence.




You can land in
a prison for up to
3 months if a pet is
abandoned
regardless of the
reason.



No animal,
including chickens
can be slaughtered
in any place other
than a
slaughterhouse.



Sick or pregnant
animals shall not
be slaughtered.






Stray dogs that
have been operated
for birth control
cannot be captured
by anybody including
any authority.


Not providing a pet
with the elements
important for survival
including access to
food, water, shelter.
Keeping it chained
for hours leads to
imprisonment.



Monkeys cannot
be owned.





Bears, monkeys,
tigers and lions
are prohibited from
being trained and
used for
entertainment
purposes.



Animal sacrifice
is illegal.







Holding or
organising an
animal fight is
a cognizable
offence.




Experimentation
and cosmetics test
on animals
is banned.




Teasing or
disturbing the
animals in a zoo and
littering the zoo
premises is an
offence punishable
by a fine or
imprisonment.


Trapping, poisoning
or baiting of any
animal leads to a
fine or an
imprisonment.




Destroying the eggs
or the nests of birds
and reptiles or
chopping a tree with
the nests of birds
and reptiles has a fine
and imprisonment.


Carrying animals
(in a vehicle) where
they are in
discomfort or in a
position causing pain
or suffering is a
punishable offence.







Such laws created by the Indian government under various acts can truly help strengthen and improve the relationship
between the nature/ environment and the mankind.

How our Future Would Look like if Animal Cruelty Continued


If animal cruelty continues in any form then,
we will soon see a world buzzing with the city sounds rather than the chirping of the birds,
a trail of cars and buildings instead of the lush trees,
and a handful of insects occupying the confined forests with half cut trees.

Hence, these rights will aid the animals to have a compartively easier survival in this daunting world and make us,
the responsible citizens of India realise our duty towards these innocent and vulnerable species.
